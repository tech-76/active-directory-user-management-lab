# Organizational Units Basics

## Purpose

Organizational Units, also called OUs, help organize Active Directory objects such as users, computers, and groups. OUs can also be used to apply Group Policy settings.

## What OUs Can Contain

OUs may contain:

- User accounts
- Computer accounts
- Groups
- Sub-OUs
- Service accounts

## Why OUs Matter

OUs help IT teams:

- Organize users by department
- Organize computers by location
- Apply Group Policy settings
- Separate admin responsibilities
- Support onboarding and offboarding
- Keep directory structure clean

## Example OU Structure

A simple organization may use OUs like:

- Users
- Computers
- Departments
- Service Accounts
- Disabled Accounts
- Admin Accounts

## Help Desk Tasks Related to OUs

- Confirm the user is in the correct OU
- Move a user to a department OU after role change
- Confirm disabled accounts are moved to the correct OU
- Check if a policy may apply because of OU placement
- Escalate if OU changes require admin approval

## Best Practices

- Follow the company's OU structure.
- Avoid moving accounts without approval.
- Keep disabled accounts organized.
- Do not create random OUs without a naming standard.
- Document account moves in the ticket.

## Example Ticket Note

Confirmed user account was located in the correct department OU. No OU changes were required.
